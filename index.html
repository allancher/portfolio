Data-driven insights

Business Case
A global player in FMCG industry had approached us to review and improve its benefit program.

Key Findings
Employee profile is majority females, with an increasing trend in dependents headcount.

Prevalent female-related neoplasm conditions amongst employees for past 3 years.

Dilemma between choosing to opt in for a major medical benefit or opting up the hospital plan.

Proposed Recommendations
As client is cost sensitive, we proposed a cost neutral approach and a cost incurring approach to solve these concerns. Based on these findings, well-founded decisions were made by client's management level to approve of our recommendations.

To meet the increasing needs of a family-oriented profile, we proposed inpatient dependent coverage for all job grades, and more childcare related items in the Flex Spending Account.

Due to high occurence of neoplasm cases, voluntary critical illness benefit is being proposed to further protect employees. Communications revolving health screening are encouraged, to create awareness for early detection and treatment.

Due to market prevalence and lower costing of providing major medical benefit, employees are now eligible for compulsory coverage to eliminate dilemma and protection against shock claims.
